The memories or enjoyable things
1.

A trip to Nami Island.

Nami Island is a small island located in Chuncheon, Gangwon Province.

It is famous for its yellow ginkgo tree-lined paths and has been used as a filming location for various TV dramas.

The island has a quiet and sentimental mood.

2.

Memories of the 2002 World Cup

the whole country was in festival mode, staying out late and roaming around, celebrating everywhere.

The 2002 World Cup marked the first time in Korean football history that the national team advanced to the semifinals.

At that time, the entire country was in a festive mood,

with people wearing red clothes and cheering together in the streets.

3.

The golden age of StarCraft.

StarCraft is an RTS game released by Blizzard Entertainment in 1998.

In the early 2000s, its popularity exploded in South Korea alongside the rapid spread of PC room.

4.

Arcade machines in front of stationery shops, and the old lady selling dalgona in front of the school.

In the early 2000s, there were many small local shops near schools.

These shops often had small arcade machines where you could play various games by inserting coins.

Although they are rare now, there used to be people who made Dalgona by hand.

Dalgona is a sweet snack made by melting sugar,

and it became popular again after appearing in the Netflix series Squid Game.

5.

Playing “Cops and Thieves” at the playground.

"Cops and Thieves" is a tag game with the concept of cops chasing thieves.

The rules are that the cops aim to catch all the thieves within a set time limit.

If they fail to catch all the thieves before time runs out, the thieves win.

6.

The World Cup, Cyworld, and the sense of anticipation for the 21st century.

Cyworld is a Korean SNS that was popular in the early 2000s.

Users could decorate their personal spaces, called Minihompy, and share diaries, photos, and guestbook entries.

It shows into what social networking was like in its early days.

7.

Back when I was an only child, before I had two younger siblings,

I remember living with my parents in a shabby,

crumbling house in an alley in Ui-dong, where the insulation was barely held together with plastic.

We all slept tightly packed into a room about around 10–13㎡.

The bathroom was across the yard, so I hated going there in winter.

I was very timid and scared to go alone at night,

so my parents bought toy walkie-talkies and stayed in constant communication with me while I went to the bathroom.

In the past, unlike today’s Korea with many apartment buildings, there were many neighborhoods made up of single family houses and small homes clustered together.

These slum-like areas were called "Daldongnae" (moon villages).

8.

Using a flip phone.

In the 2000s, before smartphones, the most common type of mobile phone was the small, thick flip phone.

9.

Unlike now, technology felt more distant from people back then.

I noticed birds in trees, telephone poles, passing cars, and people more.

Rather than looking at my phone, my eyes were drawn to everything else around me.

In the early 2000s, with digital devices and the internet not yet widespread,

people relied more on face-to-face meetings and spent more time on outdoor activities than they do now.

10.

Hanging out around college town.

In the early 2000s, Korean college town symbolized youth, with students participating in a variety of activities, including singing, drinking, and hanging out together.

For example, popular spots included Hongdae Street, Konkuk University Entrance, and Sinchon.

11.

Going to Seoul Children’s Grand Park with my family to see the animals.

Seoul Children’s Grand Park is a popular amusement park where families go for outings.

It features a zoo, botanical gardens, and various rides, attracting many visitors who come to enjoy the park with their families.

12.

New mobile devices: Nokia.

Being amazed (and a little shocked) by computer screens.

Slow internet.

Nokia was a popular Finnish mobile phone brand in the early 2000s.

Nokia phones came to symbolize mobile phones of that era.

13.

LEGO Ninjago.

Ninjago is a LEGO animated series that has been airing since 2011, depicting the adventures of ninja characters.

Its main genres are action, adventure, and fantasy.

14.

15.

Starting a bakery and going through an extremely busy period of life…

the happiest moments were when my son was born,

and later, when my daughter was born.

16.

A memory of playing tag (Escape from Hell) at the playground.

The game called “Escape from Hell” is a tag game played within a limited area using playground equipment,

where the goal is to avoid the tagger and reach a specific escape zone.

17.

A memory of going to school on a Saturday,

eating a hamburger the class president bought us,

and heading home early while soaking up the warm sunlight.

In the early 2000s, school days lasted six days a week, with a “rest Saturday” every week.

In the past, it was common for the class president or class president’s parents to bring food to share with the students in their class sometimes.
